# Westbury Design System

The single source of truth for how every Westbury Collections social post must look.
Extracted from the existing brand assets (logo + published posts). Future posts are **new,
original work in this language** — never copies of past posts, never reused text.

---

## 1. Brand essence

Westbury feels like a well-made room at dusk: warm, assured, unhurried. The design voice is
**formal but human** — a confident serif statement followed by a softer italic aside. Everything
is understated; nothing shouts. If a layout feels busy, it is wrong.

Keywords: premium · warm · British · composed · quietly witty.

---

## 2. Colour palette

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary field | Westbury Crimson | `#C0122E` | Background base (never a small accent) |
| Depth | Deep Maroon | `#3F0811` | Top of gradients, shadows |
| Seasonal dark | Plum Noir | `#1A0910` | Dark-mood variants (Halloween, evening themes) |
| Accent | Champagne Gold | `#DEB26E` | Eyebrow labels, sublines, hairline rules, small motifs |
| Watermark | Blush Pink | `#F2A8B5` | The translucent "W" only, ~25–35% opacity |
| Text | White | `#FFFFFF` | Headlines, sign-off, logo lockup |

**Rules**
- The background is **always** a crimson family gradient (Deep Maroon → Crimson, darker at top)
  or Plum Noir for seasonal dark posts. Never flat white, never off-brand colours.
- Gold is hardware, not wallpaper: use it at small scale only (labels, rules, tiny motifs).
- Add a fine film grain (±3% luminance) over every background so posts feel printed, not digital.
- Optional: a sparse scatter of tiny particles (gold/white/blush, 1–3px, low opacity) in the
  upper third — "dust in lamplight". Maximum ~25 specks; restraint is the rule.

## 3. Typography

Three voices, never more, always in this hierarchy:

| Voice | Style | Free-font equivalent | Spec |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Eyebrow** | Letterspaced serif caps, gold | Crimson Pro Regular | ~30% of headline size, tracking +60%, ALL CAPS |
| **Headline** | High-contrast Didone serif, white | Gloock / Playfair Display | 8–10% of canvas height per line, tight leading (1.15–1.2) |
| **Subline / sign-off** | Warm italic serif | Crimson Pro Italic | ~50% of headline size; gold or white |

**Hierarchy pattern (top to bottom):** eyebrow → headline → (optional gold divider) → subline.

**Headline rules**
- Short declarative sentences, 2–6 words per line, max 4 lines. Always end with a full stop.
- Sentence case ("Let the room breathe."), never all-caps headlines.
- One word may be swapped into gold italic for emphasis (see "Merry *Christmas*" pattern:
  italic gold word above/beside the white roman word).

**The gold divider** — hairline, interrupted by a single small diamond — is optional and used
on formal/celebratory posts only, centred beneath the headline.

---

## 4. Logo usage

Two logo behaviours, both usually present:

1. **Lockup (bottom-right):** "Westbury" in white bold serif with "Collections Ltd" centred
   beneath at ~44% size. Sits inside the margin, baseline-aligned with the sign-off line.
   On dark/complex art it may sit on a small crimson panel (see Halloween reference).
2. **Watermark W (architectural):** an oversized Didone "W" in Blush Pink at 25–35% opacity,
   ~55–70% of canvas height, anchored to the right side and **bleeding off the edge**.
   Text may overlap it — it is background architecture, not a foreground element.

Never: stretch, recolour outside the palette, outline, drop-shadow, or centre the logo.

---

## 5. Layout structure

- **Margins:** 8.5% of the shorter canvas edge on all sides. Nothing crosses them except the
  watermark W (which is designed to bleed).
- **Centre of gravity is low:** keep the upper quarter of the canvas open (air + particles);
  the type block sits centred on ~54% of canvas height.
- **Two layout archetypes:**
  - **A. Left-aligned** (editorial): eyebrow, headline, subline all flush left on the margin.
    Watermark W right. Used for tips, notes, seasonal statements.
  - **B. Centered** (formal): eyebrow, headline, divider, subline centred. Used for
    thank-yous, greetings, milestones.
- **Footer (every post):** italic sign-off bottom-left ("From all of us at Westbury Collections",
  or a short CTA such as "Visit the showroom this weekend."), logo lockup bottom-right.

**Canvas sizes:** Instagram portrait 1080×1350 (default) · Instagram square 1080×1080 ·
X/Twitter 1200×675.

---

## 6. Imagery & illustration

- Photography: warm, low-key interior shots; deep shadows; crimson/neutral rooms sit best.
  When photos are used, apply a Deep Maroon gradient scrim from the top so type stays legible.
- Illustration: flat, exact, geometric, in palette colours only (see the pumpkin-baubles and
  hanging-bauble references). One illustrated object per post, integrated with the type
  (e.g. objects replacing letterforms) — never clip-art scattered around.
- No stock-photo clichés, no 3D renders, no drop shadows, no gradients outside the palette.

---

## 7. Voice & copy style

- British English. Warm, brief, a little wry when the occasion allows.
- Statement + aside: the headline states; the italic line adds humanity
  ("Thank You." / *"To the team who turn up, rain or shine."*).
- Never invent products, prices, promotions or claims. Facts come only from the content
  calendar / company brief for that post.

---

## 8. Quality checklist (run before every post ships)

1. Background is a brand gradient with grain — not flat, not off-palette.
2. Exactly three type voices, correct hierarchy, margins respected.
3. Headline ≤ 4 lines, sentence case, ends with a full stop.
4. Watermark W bleeds off an edge at 25–35% opacity.
5. Sign-off bottom-left, lockup bottom-right, both inside margins.
6. Nothing overlaps illegibly; contrast of white text on background ≥ 4.5:1.
7. It would look at home next to the existing Westbury grid.
